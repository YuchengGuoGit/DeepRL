One Unifying Question

How do we scale learning systems from closed, perfect-information games to multi-agent, partially observed worlds, and eventually toward formal mathematical reasoning?

  • State representation changes.
  • Search/planning budget changes.
  • Self-play curriculum changes.
  • Evaluation and generalization standards get stricter.

AlphaGo: Hybridizing Learning + Search

  • Combined supervised pretraining, RL fine-tuning, and MCTS-guided decision making.
  • Policy network narrowed search; value network reduced rollout variance.
  • Key insight: Deep nets are strongest when embedded inside structured planning.
Talk cue (6 min): Explain why pure search or pure policy alone underperformed.

AlphaGo: Why It Was a Breakthrough

Algorithmic

Neural priors made MCTS selective and practical in a huge branching space.

Engineering

Distributed training/inference pipelines for large-scale policy and value learning.

Scientific

Demonstrated that learned evaluation can outperform handcrafted heuristics.

Cultural

Shifted mainstream belief in what deep RL systems could achieve.

AlphaGo Zero: Simplicity Through Self-Play

  • Single network outputs both policy and value.
  • No human expert data; pure self-play from random initialization.
  • MCTS targets bootstrap stronger training data over time.

Core message: remove hand-designed scaffolding, increase learning loop purity.

Talk cue (6 min): Contrast “human priors + RL” vs “tabula rasa + search-improved targets.”

AlphaGo → AlphaGo Zero: Design Transition

Before

  • Separate policy/value pipelines
  • Human data bootstrapping
  • More complex training choreography

After

  • Unified architecture
  • Pure self-play curriculum
  • Cleaner objective + iteration loop

This pattern reappears in later systems: simplification + scale + stronger environment feedback.

OpenAI Five: Scaling to Dota 2

  • Partially observed, real-time, long-horizon, high-dimensional control.
  • Massive distributed self-play generated strategic diversity.
  • Team coordination emerged from shared reward structure and co-training.
Talk cue (8 min): Emphasize the jump from turn-based perfect information to real-time imperfect information.

OpenAI Five: Key Lessons

Scale is a method

Large-scale self-play changes the optimization landscape.

Non-stationarity matters

Opponent distribution drifts; training pipelines must stabilize learning.

Evaluation must be robust

Need repeated matches and adversarial testing, not one-off wins.

Coordination is learnable

Role specialization can emerge without explicit role labels.

Quake III Arena CTF: Multi-Agent Population Training

  • Agents learned in teams with mixed teammates/opponents across a population.
  • Environment complexity forced navigation, tactics, and implicit communication.
  • Generalization assessed across unseen maps and partner combinations.
Talk cue (8 min): Describe population-based training as a curriculum over social dynamics.

From Dota to Quake: What Improved?

OpenAI Five

Single ecosystem mastery under constrained hero/rule settings.

Quake CTF

Stronger emphasis on partner/opponent diversity and transfer.

Shared lesson

Population diversity is a first-class ingredient for robust behavior.

Open problem

Reliable out-of-distribution adaptation remains difficult.

IMO-Level Formal Reasoning with RL

  • Moves from game environments to formal proof spaces.
  • Rewards linked to proof progress and correctness in formal systems.
  • Demonstrates RL-style search/control ideas in symbolic mathematics.
Talk cue (10 min): Explain why theorem proving is a strong testbed for planning + verification.

Cross-Paper Synthesis

Pattern 1

Self-play / self-improvement loops drive sustained capability growth.

Pattern 2

Search + learned priors beats either component alone in hard domains.

Pattern 3

Scale and systems engineering are core algorithmic enablers.

Pattern 4

Generalization stress tests distinguish benchmark success from robust competence.

What This Means for Future Deep RL

  • Need better sample efficiency without losing the benefits of large-scale self-play.
  • Need principled diversity mechanisms for opponents, partners, and tasks.
  • Need stronger safety/interpretability in strategic multi-agent systems.
  • Need bridges between simulation success and real-world deployment constraints.
